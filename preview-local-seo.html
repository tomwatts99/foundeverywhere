Local SEO

Show up when it matters most

When someone nearby searches for what you do, you need to be there. We make sure you are: in Google search, Google Maps, and AI tools that reference local businesses.

What is included

  • Google Business Profile optimisation

    Your GBP is often the first thing a local customer sees. We make sure it is complete, accurate, and optimised: categories, services, photos, posts, and Q and A all working together.

  • Local citation building

    Consistent NAP data across directories, review platforms, and local listings. Citations are a core local ranking signal and most businesses have inconsistencies that quietly hurt their visibility.

  • Location and business schema

    We implement LocalBusiness, PostalAddress, and GeoCoordinates schema so search engines and AI tools have structured data about exactly where you are and what you do.

  • Local keyword targeting

    We identify the searches your local customers are actually making and make sure your site and GBP are optimised to appear for them.

Who this is for

Any business that serves customers in a specific area: service businesses, retailers, hospitality, healthcare, tradespeople, and professional services. If your customers are local, local SEO is not optional.

Common questions

How long does local SEO take to show results?

Google Business Profile improvements can show within a few weeks. Organic local rankings typically improve over two to three months as citations are built and schema is indexed.

Do I need a website to do local SEO?

A website helps significantly but we can work with GBP alone as a starting point. A well optimised GBP with strong citations can drive meaningful local visibility even before a full site is in place.

Can you manage our Google Business Profile on an ongoing basis?

Yes: GBP management is included in our retainer service. Regular posts, review responses, photo updates, and performance monitoring.

Does local SEO help with AI search?

Yes. AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity increasingly reference local businesses when answering location-based queries. LocalBusiness schema and a well optimised GBP are the signals that make that happen.
